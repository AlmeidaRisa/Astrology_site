Sagar Manthan: Svarbhanu(Rahu-Ketu)

Let us understand the story behind Rahu and Ketu by understanding the infamous Samudra Manthan that is mentioned in the Vishnu Purana. The Samudra Manthana occurred after the curse of short-tempered Maharishi Durvasa to Indra Dev. All the ratnas including Amrit(the elixir Of Immortality) were sinking in an ocean and in order to retrieve them all the gods went to Lord Vishnu for help. In order to obtain Amrit the Churning of the ocean began, but this required opposite energies, hence along with gods even demons' help was required. The Mandara mountain was used to churn the ocean of milk and Vasuki naag was used as a rope between the gods and demons. 14 ratnas were extracted and alas came the Amrit. Now, everyone wanted a drop of it. But to prevent the demons from consuming it, Lord Vishnu took the avatar of a beautiful woman Mohini and asked the demons to clean themselves before having the ratna. All the demons, except one listened to Mohini. This one demon named Svarbhanu took the form of a dev and drank a drop of Amrit. When Bhagwan Vishnu realised this he tried to kill Svarbhanu with his chakra, but he was already immortal. But the chakra beheaded him forming Rahu(the head) and Ketu(the body). One must know that these are the shadows of Moon or shadow planets in Astrology. The Sun and the Moon gods were the ones who alerted about Svarbhanu's sneaking, creating a hatred between Rahu-Sun and Ketu-Moon.

Hence, people with Rahu as their ruler often tend to think differently or out of the box compared to the world around them, but things often don't go as planned for them. Just like Svarbhanu out of all the demons decided to come up with a jugaad but in the end was beheaded by Vishnu bhagvan. If Rahu is attachment then Ketu represents detachment. It is also the planet of spirituality, and people governed by ketu are normally seen to be extremely spiritual.
