The Wrath Of Mars

The story of Narasimha, that is half lion and half man, also known as the fourth form of Lord Vishnu, holds a significance and represents Mars. Hiranyakashipu, who was a demon king, deeply loathed Vishnu for killing his brother and was creating an army to defeat lord Vishnu. He also killed numerous Vishnu devotees. But the actual problem in Hiranyakashipu's life started when his own son, Prahlad turned out to be an admirer of Vishnu. In his childhood somebody had narrated a parable of Vishnu to Prahlad and ever since he started worshipping. As he was opposing his father's ideologies, firstly Hiranyakashipu tried stopping him and later on took steps to punish and even kill him. But everytime lord Vishnu would save the child. Furious, Hiranyakashipu asks Prahalad where his Vishnu is, to which Prahlad replies,"he is everywhere, including the pillar behind you". An angry Hiranyakashipu breaks the pillar just to find Vishnu in his Narasimha avatar, who is extremely angry at Hiranyakashipu. This powerful avatar kills the demon king by tearing his stomach from the center.

This avatar is related to Mars, as just like Narsimha, if Mars gets clarity in his life then he can drive his entire energy, aggression, and masculine power towards achieving it. It is a very angry planet.
